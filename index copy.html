Top Five Books I Read in the Past Six Months (Continously Updated)

Originals: How Non-Conformists Move the World - Adam Grant

The Wharton Professor, Adam Grant, once again delivered a master piece. This time on how to be an innovator. He took lessons from MLK Jr., Seinfeld, Kodak, and many others and explained how many innovations actually came from sedoulous preparation and years of experience, not just a moment of brillance struck from pure genius.

The Innovator's Delimma - Clayton M. Christensen

This book was argubly the legendary Harvard Business School professor's most important piece work. This book lead to a complete revamp of innovation strategy. This is a must read for anyone who is trying to build a sustainable business in this rapidly evolving world.

The Subtle Art of Not Giving a Fuck: A Counterintuitive Approach to Living a Good Life - Mark Manson

Today's world is the most peaceful, conveient, and richest world of since the beginning of human existence. This is also the most stressful and dreadful world. Mark Mason uses his modern stoicsim to make sense of this world.

Blink: The Power of Thinking Without Thinking - Malcom Gladwell

Malcom Gladwell uses his insightfulness to uncover the power of making snap decisions. While at the same time, investigates the dark side and egregious results of making snap judgements.

The Internet of Money: Volume one - Andreas M. Antonopoulos

In this first book of a three volume series, Andreas Antonopoulos tackles the commmon misconceptions of cryptocurrency. He also presented arguments for how cryptocurrency will never be stopped, and envisons a much more conveient and data-secure future with the adoption of blockchain technology.
